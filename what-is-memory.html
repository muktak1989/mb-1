What is the role of memory in our daily lives?

The job of your brain is to help you process and understand the information you encounter every day, and your memories are the tools that help you make sense of it all. Your brain is made up of billions of cells called neurons, and each one of these cells is responsible for storing information. This is why you can remember your childhood home, but not the address of the office you’re reading this article from right now. Your neurons are like tiny filing cabinets that store information about everything you see, hear, smell, taste, and touch. When you need to remember something, your brain uses these stored memories to help you recall it. For example, if you see your best friend from high school at the grocery store and are trying to remember her name, your brain will use the information it has about her to help you recall it. This is why you can remember your high school best friend, but not your neighbor’s name.
